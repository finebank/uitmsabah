5.1 Basics of Insurance/Takaful

5.1.1 Definition and Importance of Insurance/Takaful

Insurance

Insurance is a contract between an insurer and an insured, where the insurer agrees to pay a certain amount of money to the insured or their beneficiaries in the event of a specified loss or damage.

The insured pays a premium to the insurer in exchange for this protection. Insurance can cover various types of risks, such as life, health, property, liability, and travel.

Takaful

Takaful is a form of Islamic insurance that is based on the principles of cooperation and solidarity. Takaful participants contribute to a pool of funds that is used to help each other in case of misfortune or calamity.

Takaful operates according to the Shariah law, which prohibits interest, gambling, and uncertainty. Takaful can also cover various types of risks, such as family, medical, motor, fire, and marine.

Importance of Insurance/Takaful

Financial Security

Provides financial security and peace of mind to the insured or their beneficiaries in case of unforeseen events that may cause loss or harm.

Reduces Social Burden

Helps reduce the burden on society and the government by transferring the risk from the individual to the collective.

Economic Development

Encourages economic development and social welfare by facilitating trade, investment, and entrepreneurship.

Insurance Penetration in Malaysia

According to a report by PwC Malaysia (2023), Malaysia has a low insurance/takaful penetration rate compared to other countries in the region. The report estimated that only 56% of Malaysians have some form of insurance/takaful coverage, while the average penetration rate in Southeast Asia is 68%.

Basic Principles Governing Insurance Contracts

Utmost Good Faith

Both parties must disclose all material facts. The insured must provide truthful information, and the insurer must clearly explain the terms and conditions.

Insurable Interest

The insured must have a financial interest in the subject matter of insurance. You can only insure something if its loss would cause you financial hardship.

Indemnity

The insurer promises to restore the insured to the same financial position as before the loss occurred, no more and no less.

Subrogation

After paying a claim, the insurer gets the right to pursue recovery from third parties responsible for the loss, preventing the insured from claiming twice.

Basic Risk Management

Risk Identification

Recognizing potential risks and their sources

Risk Assessment

Analyzing the likelihood and impact of risks

Risk Control

Implementing measures to reduce risks

Risk Transfer

Shifting risk responsibility to another party (e.g., insurance)

5.2 Types of Insurance/Takaful

Insurance and takaful products come in various types to protect different aspects of our lives. Understanding these types helps you choose the right coverage for your needs.

Life Insurance/Family Takaful

Life insurance is a contract between an individual (the policyholder) and an insurance company. The policyholder pays regular premiums, and in return, the insurance company provides a lump sum payment, known as the death benefit, to the policyholder's beneficiaries upon their demise.

Family takaful similarly provides financial protection to participants' beneficiaries in the event of their demise, but operates based on Islamic principles of mutual cooperation and shared responsibility.

Types of Life Insurance/Family Takaful

Term Life Insurance/Takaful

Provides coverage for a specified period (term), typically 10, 20, or 30 years. If the insured person dies during the term, beneficiaries receive the death benefit. No cash value accumulation.

Whole Life Insurance/Takaful

Provides coverage for the entire lifetime with a death benefit and savings component (cash value). Premiums are higher but remain constant. Can be used for estate planning.

Endowment Insurance/Takaful

Provides a lump sum payment at a specific date or upon death/permanent disability (whichever occurs first). Has both savings and death benefit components.

Investment-Linked Insurance/Takaful

Combines insurance coverage with investment opportunities. Part of premiums are invested in different funds chosen by the policyholder based on risk appetite.

Special Types
Family Takaful

Provides coverage for the entire family under one contract and pays a benefit upon death or permanent disability of any family member.

Mortgage Reducing Term Assurance/Takaful

Provides decreasing coverage over time to match mortgage balance. Pays benefit to settle outstanding mortgage upon death/disability.

General Insurance/Takaful

General insurance/takaful covers losses or damage caused by various risks such as fire, theft, accident, natural disaster, or liability. It may cover property, vehicles, travel, personal accident, or business.

Some products also offer additional benefits such as roadside assistance, towing service, or legal advice.

Types of General Insurance/Takaful

Motor Vehicle Insurance/Takaful

Covers loss or damage to your vehicle due to accident, fire, theft, etc. Also covers liability for bodily injury or property damage to third parties.

Fire Insurance/Takaful

Covers loss or damage to property due to fire, lightning, explosion, etc. Can also cover loss of rent or income due to business interruption.

Homeowners Insurance/Takaful

Covers loss or damage to your house and contents due to various causes like fire, theft, flood, earthquake, etc.

Personal Accident Insurance/Takaful

Covers death or disability due to accident. Can also cover medical expenses and loss of income due to accidents.

Travel Insurance/Takaful

Covers various risks during travel, including medical expenses, loss of baggage, flight delay, cancellation, etc.

Liability Insurance/Takaful

Covers legal liability for bodily injury or property damage to third parties caused by your negligence or wrongful act.

Medical & Health Insurance/Takaful

Medical and health insurance/takaful covers the medical expenses incurred by the policyholder or participant due to illness or injury. It may also provide benefits such as hospitalization allowance, daily cash benefit, or funeral expenses.

Some products also offer critical illness coverage, where a lump sum amount is paid upon diagnosis of a specified critical illness.

Types of Medical & Health Insurance/Takaful

Hospitalization and Surgical Insurance

Helps alleviate financial burden associated with hospital stays, surgeries, and medical procedures, including anesthesia and post-operative care.

Critical Illness Insurance

Provides a lump sum benefit upon diagnosis of any specified illnesses (typically covers 36 major conditions). Helps cover medical costs and loss of income.

Hospital Income Insurance

Pays specified sum daily, weekly, or monthly (subject to annual limit) during hospital stay due to covered illness or injury.

Disability Income Insurance

Provides income stream to replace portion of pre-disability income when unable to work due to sickness or injury.

Medical Card

A medical card is a common type of health insurance/takaful product in Malaysia. It allows you to access medical services at panel hospitals or clinics without paying upfront. The insurer or takaful operator pays medical bills directly to the hospital according to policy terms.

Principles of Takaful

Takaful is an Islamic alternative to conventional insurance, adhering to Shariah principles. It operates on the concept of mutual cooperation and shared responsibility among participants.

Key Principles:

  • Tabarru': Participants contribute to a common fund, known as the Tabarru' fund, which is used to provide benefits to those in need.

  • Mudarabah: The Tabarru' fund is managed by a takaful operator as the Mudarib, who invests the funds in Shariah-compliant investments.

Types of Takaful Policies:

  • Family Takaful: Similar to life insurance, provides financial protection to beneficiaries in the event of death.

  • General Takaful: Covers non-life risks such as property, motor, and travel.

5.3 Insurance/Takaful Claims

One of the most important aspects of insurance and takaful is the claim process. A claim is a request by the insured or the participant to receive compensation or benefit from the insurer or the takaful operator for a loss or damage that is covered by the policy or the certificate.

The Claims Process

Step 1: Report the Loss

Report the loss or damage to your insurer or takaful operator as soon as possible and provide relevant information and documents.

Most insurance policies require you to report a claim within a specific timeframe, often 24-48 hours for urgent matters.

Keep copies of all claim-related documents for your records.

Step 2: Fill Out a Claim Form

Complete and submit a claim form along with supporting evidence, such as receipts, invoices, police reports, or medical reports.

Step 3: Cooperate with Investigation

Work with the insurer or takaful operator during their investigation and assessment, providing any additional information or documents if requested.

The insurer may send an adjuster to evaluate the damage in person, especially for property claims.

The payment may be subject to deductibles or excess amounts as specified in your policy.

Step 4: Receive Payment

Receive the payment or benefit from the insurer or takaful operator, either by cheque, bank transfer, or other methods.

Guidelines for Successful Claims

Understand Your Policy

Read and understand your policy or certificate carefully and know what is covered and what is not.

Keep Important Documents

Keep your policy or certificate in a safe place and have a copy handy when making a claim.

Act Quickly

Notify your insurer or takaful operator immediately after a loss or damage occurs and follow their instructions.

Be Accurate and Complete

Provide accurate and complete information and documents when making a claim and avoid making false or exaggerated claims.

Document Everything

Keep all receipts and records related to your claim and be prepared to answer any questions from your insurer or takaful operator.

Be Patient and Cooperative

Be patient and cooperative during the claim process and respect the decision of your insurer or takaful operator.

Resources for Help with Claims

Insurance/Takaful Agent

Can assist with claim forms and liaise with the insurer/operator
Insurance/Takaful Broker

Can advise on rights/obligations and negotiate with insurer/operator
Legal Adviser

Can represent you in case of legal issues or disputes with claims
Ombudsman for Financial Services

Can mediate between you and insurer/operator if dissatisfied with decision
Bank Negara Malaysia

Regulates and supervises the insurance/takaful industry in Malaysia

Common Claim Issues to Avoid

Late Notification

Delayed reporting of claims can result in denial of coverage. Report incidents promptly.

Insufficient Documentation

Lack of supporting evidence can delay or reduce claim payments. Keep detailed records.

Misrepresentation

Providing false information can result in claim denial and policy cancellation. Be honest.

Policy Exclusions

Failing to understand policy exclusions can lead to unexpected claim denials. Read your policy carefully.

Chapter Summary

Key Takeaways

  • Insurance and takaful provide financial protection against unexpected events and help individuals manage risks effectively.

  • Different types of insurance/takaful products cater to different needs and risks, including life, general, and medical/health insurance.

  • Takaful operates according to Shariah principles based on mutual cooperation and shared responsibility.

  • The claims process involves reporting the loss, submitting a claim form, cooperating with the investigation, and receiving payment.
