What is Personal Financial Planning?

Definition

Personal financial planning is the process of setting and reaching your personal goals by using your money effectively. It involves:

  • Deciding what you want to achieve in life
  • Determining how much money you need to reach your goals
  • Creating budgets for income and expenses
  • Developing savings and investment strategies
  • Managing debt effectively
  • Protecting your assets from risks

The Six-Step Financial Planning Process

Assess Current Financial Foothold

Gather information about your income, expenses, assets, liabilities, and risk tolerance.

  • Income sources and amounts
  • Expenses and spending patterns
  • Assets (what you own)
  • Liabilities (what you owe)
  • Risk tolerance and time horizon

Establish Financial Goals

Set specific and measurable outcomes for the short, medium, and long term.

SMART Goals: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

Identify Alternative Courses of Action

Brainstorm different strategies to achieve your goals, considering:

  • Your income and expenses
  • Your assets and liabilities
  • Your risk tolerance and time horizon
  • Pros and cons of each option

Evaluate Alternatives

Choose the best strategy with the highest reward for acceptable risk.

Use tools like decision matrices or cost-benefit analysis

Implement Financial Action Plan

Take concrete actions to execute your chosen strategy:

  • Set up savings or investment accounts
  • Automate savings and investments
  • Track progress regularly
  • Celebrate achievements

Review and Revise

Periodically review your plan and adjust as needed, especially when:

  • Life circumstances change
  • Financial situation changes
  • Market conditions change
  • Professional advice is needed

Types of Personal Financial Goals

Short-Term Goals

Achievable within 1 year

  • Building emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
  • Paying off high-interest credit card debt
  • Saving for vacation
  • Creating and sticking to budget

Intermediate-Term Goals

Achievable within 1-5 years

  • Saving for house down payment
  • Paying off student/car loans
  • Saving for education/skills
  • Starting a small business

Long-Term Goals

Achievable in 5+ years

  • Achieving financial independence
  • Retirement planning (EPF + private)
  • Paying off mortgage
  • Building wealth through investments
  • Estate planning and legacy

The SMART Framework

S

Specific

Goals should be clear and well-defined, not vague

M

Measurable

Goals should be quantifiable so progress can be tracked

A

Achievable

Goals should be realistic and attainable with your resources

R

Relevant

Goals should align with your values and overall financial plan

T

Time-bound

Goals should have a deadline to create urgency and focus

SMART Goal Examples

Not SMART

"I want to save money for a house."

SMART Version

"I will save RM50,000 for a house down payment in 5 years by depositing RM833 monthly into a high-yield savings account."

Not SMART

"I want to reduce my debt."

SMART Version

"I will pay off my RM10,000 credit card debt in 18 months by making RM600 monthly payments and not using the card for new purchases."

Key Takeaways

Essential for Success
