Understanding Taxes

Taxes are mandatory payments that individuals and businesses make to the government. They fund public services and goods such as education, healthcare, infrastructure, and defense. These are benefits that we, as a society, cannot individually provide.

Taxes also aid the government in achieving social and economic objectives like reducing inequality, promoting growth, and protecting the environment. However, taxes are not only crucial for society as a whole but also for your personal financial planning.

They influence your income, expenses, savings, investments, and debt. Therefore, understanding the basic concepts of personal taxation and planning your tax strategy is essential.

Key Takeaway

Understand how taxes impact every aspect of your financial life to make informed decisions about your money.

How Taxes Affect Your Personal Finances

Let's explore some of the ways taxes impact your personal finances:

1

Disposable Income

Taxes reduce your disposable income, which is the amount of money left after paying taxes. This reduction means you have less money to spend or save for your future goals.

2

Cash Flow

Taxes affect your cash flow, the difference between your income and expenses. They increase your expenses and reduce your income, leaving you with less money to manage your budget, pay off debts, or invest in assets.

3

Investment Returns

Taxes influence your investment returns, the profits or losses from your investments. They reduce your profits or increase your losses, leaving you with less money to grow your wealth or achieve your financial objectives.

4

Tax Liability

Taxes can reduce or increase your tax liability depending on your financial decisions. They can vary depending on the type, amount, and timing of your income and expenses.

Understanding Taxable Income

Taxable income is the amount of income subject to tax by the government. It can come from various sources such as employment, self-employment, investment, or other income. Taxable income is significant because it determines your tax liability and what deductions or exemptions you can claim.

Sources of Taxable Income

This includes salary, wages, bonuses, commissions, tips, allowances, or benefits. Employment income is taxed at progressive rates, meaning the higher your income, the higher the tax rate. You can claim deductions for certain employment-related expenses.

This includes profits, fees, charges, or royalties from running your own business or freelancing. Self-employment income is taxed at a flat rate of 15% for resident individuals and 30% for non-resident individuals. You can claim deductions for certain business or freelance work-related expenses.

This includes dividends, interest, rent, or capital gains from investing in assets such as stocks, bonds, real estate, properties, or businesses. Investment income is taxed at different rates depending on the type of investment and the holding period. You can claim exemptions for certain investment-related incomes.

This includes pensions, annuities, alimony, prizes, winnings, or inheritance. Other income is taxed at different rates depending on the source and the amount of income. You can claim exemptions for certain incomes related to other sources.

Important Note

Not all income is taxable. Understanding what counts as taxable income can help you properly report your income and avoid penalties. When in doubt, consult with a tax professional.

Tax Exemptions

Tax exemptions are certain types of income that are not subject to tax by the government. These exemptions are granted for incomes that are considered beneficial for society or the economy. By strategically planning and claiming these exemptions, individuals can significantly enhance their financial wellbeing.

Key Personal Tax Exemptions in Malaysia

Dividends

Dividends from Malaysian companies or cooperatives are exempt from tax.

Interest

Interest from Malaysian government securities or approved banks is exempt from tax.

Pensions

Pensions from approved funds or schemes are exempt from tax.

Gifts

Gifts from relatives or employers are exempt from tax.

Scholarships

Scholarships for education or research are exempt from tax.

Leave Passage

Tax exemption for 3 local leave passages yearly and 1 overseas leave passage (max RM38,000) annually.

Medical and Dental Benefits

Medical benefits provided by employers including maternity expenses and traditional medicine are exempt.

Retirement Gratuity

Full gratuity amount is exempt based on specific conditions regarding retirement age and service duration.

Pro Tip

Keep necessary documentation for all potential tax exemptions. Evidence of eligibility can save you significant amounts during tax filing season.

Tax Reliefs

Tax reliefs are designed to reduce the taxable income of individuals, thereby lowering the amount of tax owed. These reliefs can be claimed for a wide array of expenditures and contributions, ranging from education and medical expenses to lifestyle purchases and retirement savings. By strategically planning and claiming these reliefs, individuals can significantly enhance their financial wellbeing.

Tax Rebates

Tax rebates are amounts that are subtracted from your tax payable to reduce your tax liability. Tax rebates can help you save money on taxes and increase your disposable income.

Individual Rebate

RM400 for resident individuals whose chargeable income does not exceed RM35,000.

Apply if you're a resident individual with chargeable income of RM35,000 or less.

Spouse Rebate

RM400 for resident individuals whose spouse has no income or elects for joint assessment.

Claim if your spouse has no income or agrees to combine incomes for tax purposes.

Child Rebate

RM200 for each unmarried child under 18 years old who is receiving full-time education.

Available for each eligible child in full-time education at a recognized institution.

Important Reminder

To claim tax rebates, you need to meet the eligibility criteria and provide the necessary information and documents. Make sure to fill in the appropriate forms and submit them to the IRBM.

    Tax Donations / Gifts

    Tax donations are charitable contributions that you make to approved organizations or causes. Tax donations can reduce your taxable income and your tax liability. They can also support social welfare and development.

    Types of Tax-Deductible Donations

    Cash Donations to Approved Institutions

    Monetary contributions to approved institutions such as schools, universities, hospitals, mosques, temples, churches, or foundations.

    Deduction: Up to 10% of aggregate income

    Gifts to Government for Public Purposes

    Gifts of money or assets to government, state government, or local authorities for public purposes such as education, healthcare, infrastructure, or defense.

    Deduction: Up to 10% of aggregate income

    Gifts of Artifacts or Cultural Items

    Gifts of artifacts, paintings, manuscripts, or antiques to the government or approved institutions for public display.

    Deduction: Up to 10% of aggregate income

    Tip for Donors

    Always request and keep official receipts for your donations. These serve as proof during tax filing and potential audits. Additionally, verify that the organization is approved for tax-deductible donations before contributing.

    Tax Offenses and Penalties

    In Malaysia, tax offenses are taken seriously and can result in significant penalties. Understanding these offenses and their consequences can help you avoid legal troubles and financial losses.

    Common Tax Offenses

    Failure to File Taxes

    If your annual income exceeds the taxable threshold, it is mandatory to report your entire income to the Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia (IRBM).

    Penalty: Fine of RM200 to RM20,000, imprisonment (up to six months), or both.

    Underreporting Income

    Claiming that you earn a lower income than you actually do is a serious offense.

    Penalty: Fine of RM1,000 to RM10,000, plus 200% of the tax amount that was undercharged.

    Claiming Tax Reliefs Excessively

    Overstating your tax reliefs just to get reduced tax charges.

    Penalty: Fine of RM300 to RM10,000, imprisonment, or both.

    Failure to Maintain Proper Records

    Not keeping proper records of your income and expenses.

    Penalty: Various penalties depending on severity.

    Tax Evasion

    Wilfully and intentionally evading tax or assisting any other person to evade tax.

    Penalty: Fine of RM1,000 to RM20,000, imprisonment (up to three years), or both, and 300% of the tax undercharged.

    Assisting Others in Tax Evasion

    If you assist or advise others to under declare their income without reasonable care.

    Penalty: Fine of RM1,000 to RM20,000, imprisonment for a term not exceeding 3 years, or both, and 300% of the tax undercharged.

    Warning

    Tax evasion is not only financially costly due to penalties but can also damage your reputation and potentially result in criminal charges. Always consult with a tax professional if you're unsure about how to properly report your income or claim deductions.
