Types of Investment Vehicles

Cash & Fixed Deposits

Cash and fixed deposits are the most liquid and low-risk investment vehicles. They involve depositing money in a bank account or a financial institution and earning interest over a fixed period.

Key Features:

  • High liquidity and accessibility
  • Capital preservation with minimal risk
  • Predictable, fixed returns
  • Low returns that may not keep pace with inflation
  • Limited growth potential compared to other investments

Government & Corporate Bonds

Bonds are debt securities that represent a loan from an investor to a borrower, such as a government or a corporation. The borrower promises to pay back the principal amount plus interest at a specified date in the future.

Government Bonds

Issued by federal or state governments to finance budget deficits or development projects.

  • • Malaysian Government Securities (MGS)
  • • Government Investment Issues (GII)
  • • Malaysian Treasury Bills (MTB)
  • • Malaysian Islamic Treasury Bills (MITB)
  • • Malaysian Savings Bonds (MSB)

Corporate Bonds

Issued by private or public companies to raise funds for business operations or expansion plans.

  • • Medium-Term Notes (MTN)
  • • Islamic Medium Term Notes (IMTN)
  • • Commercial Papers (CP)
  • • Islamic Commercial Papers (ICP)
  • • Asset-Backed Securities (ABS)
  • • Sukuk (Islamic bonds)

Mutual Funds & Exchange Traded Funds

Mutual funds and exchange traded funds (ETFs) are pooled investment vehicles that allow investors to diversify their portfolios by investing in a collection of securities, such as stocks, bonds, commodities, or currencies.

Mutual Funds

Open-ended funds that issue and redeem units based on the net asset value (NAV) of the fund at the end of each trading day.

  • Professional management
  • Diversification across assets
  • Wide variety of investment strategies
  • Higher expense ratios
  • Only priced once per day

Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)

Closed-ended funds that trade on stock exchanges like stocks at market prices that may differ from the NAV.

  • Lower expense ratios
  • Trade throughout the day
  • More tax-efficient
  • Bid-ask spreads when trading
  • Fewer active management options

Malaysian Fund Market

According to the Securities Commission Malaysia, there were 212 mutual funds with a total NAV of RM478 billion and 18 ETFs with a total NAV of RM3 billion in Malaysia as of March 2021.

Equities (Common & Preferred Stock)

Equities are shares of ownership in a company that entitle investors to a proportionate share of the company's assets, earnings, and dividends. Equities are the most risky but also potentially the most rewarding investment vehicles.

Common Stock

The most common type of equity that gives investors voting rights and residual claims on the company's assets and earnings.

  • Voting rights in company decisions
  • Potential for dividends (not guaranteed)
  • Potential for capital appreciation
  • Highest risk among equity holders
  • Last claim on assets in bankruptcy

Preferred Stock

A hybrid type of equity that gives investors fixed dividends and priority claims but no voting rights.

  • Fixed dividend payments
  • Priority over common stockholders for dividends
  • Higher claim on assets in bankruptcy
  • No voting rights
  • Limited potential for capital appreciation

Steps for Effective Investment Planning

1

Define Your Goals & Objectives

Just as building a house requires a clear understanding of what you want to achieve, start by identifying your financial goals and objectives.

  • Identify short, medium, and long-term financial goals
  • Quantify goals with specific amounts and timeframes
  • Prioritize goals based on importance and urgency
2

Assess Your Risk Tolerance

Similar to considering the architectural style and location of a house, understanding your risk tolerance is essential in investment planning.

  • Consider your emotional comfort with market volatility
  • Evaluate your financial capacity to absorb losses
  • Take risk tolerance assessments or consult with a professional
3

Determine Your Time Horizon

Just like considering the construction timeline of a house, identify your investment time horizon for each financial goal.

  • Short-term: Less than 3 years
  • Medium-term: 3-7 years
  • Long-term: More than 7 years
4

Build a Diversified Portfolio

Just as a well-designed house requires a mix of materials and components, a diversified investment portfolio is vital for managing risk and maximizing returns.

  • Spread investments across different asset classes
  • Diversify within each asset class (sectors, geographies)
  • Align asset allocation with risk tolerance and time horizon
5

Conduct Thorough Research

Like researching the quality and reputation of contractors or suppliers before starting construction, it is crucial to research potential investment options.

  • Analyze historical performance and trends
  • Evaluate fund management teams or company fundamentals
  • Consider fees, expense ratios, and tax implications
6

Monitor & Review Regularly

Similar to conducting regular inspections during construction, regularly monitor and review your investments to ensure they align with your goals.

  • Review portfolio performance quarterly or annually
  • Rebalance portfolio to maintain target asset allocation
  • Adjust strategy as life circumstances or goals change
7

Stay Disciplined & Patient

Just as it takes time to build a house, investing for the long term requires discipline and patience to achieve your financial goals.

  • Avoid emotional reactions to market fluctuations
  • Stick to your investment plan and strategy
  • Focus on long-term goals rather than short-term volatility

Expert Investment Planning Tips

Start Early

Time is one of the most powerful factors in investing. The earlier you start, the more time your investments have to grow through compounding.

Invest Consistently

Regular investing through techniques like dollar-cost averaging can reduce the impact of market volatility and build wealth steadily over time.

Protect Your Downside

Always have an emergency fund and appropriate insurance coverage before investing. This protects your investments from being liquidated in emergencies.

Minimize Fees

Investment fees can significantly impact your returns over time. Pay attention to expense ratios, transaction costs, and management fees.

Educate Yourself

Continuously learn about investing principles, markets, and financial planning. Knowledge is your best defense against poor investment decisions.

Seek Professional Advice

Consider consulting with a qualified financial advisor, especially for complex financial situations or when you're just starting out.
